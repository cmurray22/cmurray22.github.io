currency of exchange

Seashells, tea leaves, macaw feathers, and bats' teeth have all been used as money. Money works because people believe in its value.

Now, the Bank of Canada creates money --- seemingly out of nothing, but it's based on the expectation that citizens will produce goods and services. Goods and services have Real value.

We all believe in the value of money. Problem, the way it gets distributed maintains "scarcity". Many people can't get enough money to prosper.

Conservative cutbacks are coming, prices will keep going up, and billionaires get most of the money that is created.

But any group can create its own currency of exchange among themselves. Often they name their currencies "Green Dollars" or "Hamilton Dollars".

I didn't want to use "Dollars" but it might be better than my term "Credits?" What do you think? We could call it anything. I thought about "Coin" or "Booty" or "Nice Dinero" or "Beans"....

Maybe if we get some bats' teeth to exchange, people might be more interested ...?

We all want money -- why? To buy something. If we can get the "something" with NTAL credits instead of money, we're good.

Though the benefit of local currency is small relative to the total cost of living, it's a way of reclaiming some power over your ability to take part in economic activity. And helping others in the community.

What often happens is people get things with credit exchange that they just wouldn't have the money to buy. Like art, or Reiki....

As our group grows we will get more items or services that are attractive to people. Food is attractive.
